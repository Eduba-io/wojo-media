The structural read

Wojo's site advertises the 82% Jason-effect as a feature. At 50 staff that pattern recognition is a moat. At 150 it is the ceiling. Every new hire onboards clients at a lower success rate than the founder. Compound that across 100 more hires and the Trustpilot tail gets longer while the topline goal stays the same distance away.

The Meta Advantage+ and Google Performance Max releases over the last twelve months are eating the floor of the paid-media services market. The agencies that survive the next 24 months are the ones that codify the founder's pattern recognition and move up-stack before the bottom commodifies.

The Eduba frame, applied to the Wojo stack

60%Traditional work

Playbook documentation. Role-and-responsibility mapping. Intake-to-handoff SLAs. CRM and project-management hygiene that Wojo is too busy closing deals to write down. Database work and process work. Not AI work.

30%Rule-based logic

Scoring inputs at the qualification call. Routing rules for the operating chief's queue. Conditional gates Jason runs in his head but nobody else can see. Postgres and a clean decision tree get this done.

10%Genuine AI orchestration

A methodology context layer that captures the Jason-effect in something a junior strategist queries during the first three calls. A multi-agent QA layer that catches the pattern mismatches that today show up as Trustpilot complaints two months later. This is where the LLM earns its keep.

Agencies that skip this division burn budget building generative tools on top of a knowledge base nobody has written. The order matters. Codify the methodology first. AI orchestration sits on top of that, never under it.

Four concrete builds for Wojo

These are the deliverables Eduba would scope into the engagement. Each one closes a specific gap between the 50-person Wojo and the 150-person Wojo.

01 · Methodology context

The Jason-effect onboarding co-pilot

A real-time context layer a junior strategist queries during the first three calls. Trained on Jason's actual call recordings and pattern decisions. Surfaces the next question Jason would ask, the fork Jason would identify, the disqualifier Jason would catch.

Target outcomeJunior strategists onboarding at 70%+ of the founder's success rate by month 3, not month 18.
02 · Ethics Engine, applied to brand voice

Brand-DNA psychometric audit on every creative

Eduba's published Ethics Engine evaluates the moral and ideological patterns in LLM output. Pointed at marketing, the same mechanism builds a psychometric brand-DNA profile per client and audits every ad creative against it before launch. Human-drafted or LLM-generated. Catches tonal drift, off-brand moral-foundation appeals, and version-three slippage.

What it locks downThe "we sound like you, not us" promise becomes audit-grade enforceable across 150 strategists.
03 · Multi-agent orchestration

The ad-concept QA pipeline

Strategist drafts a concept in the CRM. The pipeline runs it through brand-DNA audit, performance prediction against the client's historical campaign pattern, Meta and Google policy compliance check, and a final flagged-issue report for the human reviewer.

Target outcome4-6 hours per strategist per week recovered. Pattern mismatches caught before launch instead of two months after.
04 · ICM, applied to agency ops

The methodology context layer

Wojo's institutional knowledge encoded as a layered, queryable filesystem. L0 identity (who Wojo is, who Wojo will not work with). L1 segment patterns. L2 channel patterns. L3 campaign archetypes. L4 active client working files. The methodology becomes the operating system.

What it standardizesEvery strategist queries the same context, runs the same checks, hits the same standard.
