The structural read

Wojo's site advertises the 82% Jason-effect as a feature. At 50 staff that pattern recognition is a moat. At 150 it is the ceiling. Every new hire onboards clients at a lower success rate than the founder. Compound that across 100 more hires and the Trustpilot tail gets longer while the topline goal stays the same distance away.

The Meta Advantage+ and Google Performance Max releases over the last twelve months are eating the floor of the paid-media services market. The agencies that survive the next 24 months are the ones that codify the founder's pattern recognition and move up-stack before the bottom commodifies.

The Eduba frame, applied to the Wojo stack

60%Traditional work

Playbook documentation. Role-and-responsibility mapping. Intake-to-handoff SLAs. CRM and project-management hygiene that Wojo is too busy closing deals to write down. Database work and process work. Not AI work.

30%Rule-based logic

Scoring inputs at the qualification call. Routing rules for the operating chief's queue. Conditional gates Jason runs in his head but nobody else can see. Postgres and a clean decision tree get this done.

10%Genuine AI orchestration

A methodology context layer that captures the Jason-effect in something a junior strategist queries during the first three calls. A multi-agent QA layer that catches the pattern mismatches that today show up as Trustpilot complaints two months later. This is where the LLM earns its keep.

Agencies that skip this division burn budget building generative tools on top of a knowledge base nobody has written. The order matters. Codify the methodology first. AI orchestration sits on top of that, never under it.
